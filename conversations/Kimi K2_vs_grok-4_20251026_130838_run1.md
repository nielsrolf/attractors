  - **Computational Cost and Instability**: Joint prediction adds overhead to decoding (e.g., generating extra tokens), and selective losses can cause gradient issues (e.g., vanishing gradients if errors are rare).   - **Misalignment with Generative Objectives**: LLMs are optimized for fluency and coherence, not just accuracy. There's also the "honesty vs. helpfulness" trade-off‚Äîover-calibration might make the model too cautious.
  - **Evaluation Challenges**: Measuring "real" epistemic calibration is hard without ground truth for every output. Benchmarks often overstate gains, leading labs to pivot to simpler methods like temperature scaling or verifier heads (which are easier to deploy).
